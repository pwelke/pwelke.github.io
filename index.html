Pascal Welke

I am an Assistant Professor in Data Science at Lancaster University Leipzig and I am affiliated with the Machine Learning research unit at TU Wien. I received my PhD from the University of Bonn. After a PostDoc there I moved to TU Wien before joining LU Leipzig in early 2025. In my research, I investigate learning on and with graphs. In particular, I combine neural networks, graph mining, and graph theory to develop and analyze expressive graph representations, as well as efficient similarity based learning on graphs. I regularly publish and participate in top ML conferences such as NeurIPS, ICML, or AAAI and organize workshops and regular seminars for the graph learning community.

I also teach undergraduate and graduate courses in Computer Science and I supervise BA and MA theses on a regular basis.

My Erdős number is at most 3 (via Torsten Suel and Endre Szemerédi).
